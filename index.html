OASIS ePR - Electronic Patient Records

A lightweight, offline-first Electronic Patient Record (EPR) system in its early stages of development, designed to address the critical challenges of patient data management within Ghana's healthcare infrastructure

Motivation

The current reliance on paper-based records in many Ghanaian clinics and hospitals leads to fragmented patient histories, inefficient workflows, and critical data being lost or inaccessible during patient transfers or follow-up care. This project is motivated by the urgent need for a centralized, reliable, and scalable digital system that works even in environments with limited or intermittent internet connectivity

How it Helps

  • Provides a single, unified patient record that can be securely accessed by authorized healthcare providers across different facilities, ensuring a complete medical history is available for informed decision-making.
  • Digitizes patient registration, clinical notes, and prescription tracking, reducing administrative burdens and allowing medical staff to focus on patient care
  • Anonymized and aggregated data will help public health officials track disease outbreaks, monitor treatment outcomes, and allocate resources more effectively
